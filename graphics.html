graphics

Vision of the 20's

A digital painting created after months of research on the fashion and makeup styles in the 1920s. I started the process by looking at photographs of prominent women in the 1920s. I learned that in a way, the popular fashion played a role in influencing art. I based my painting after the Maybelline Illustrations.

ACTE Logo

Logo design for the 2015 Association for Career and Technical Education Pin Contest. This was one of my first logo designs so I learned about how shapes and colors can contribute to the message of the brand. For example, the flat geometric design shows that the company is modern.

Brian Head Art Festival

A poster created for the 2015 Brian Head Arts Festival. I researched the iconic Brian Head landmarks and the festival itself to see what it offered. I was also busy working on another project so working on this poster taught me about how to balance my work and sort my priorities. This poster placed 10th and was displayed for the community to vote on the "People's Choice" Award.

Class of 2017 T-Shirt

A submission for the 2015-2016 Junior Year T-Shirt Design content. Our mascot is the Coyoto and our class slogan is "Kings and Queens of 2017" so the crown represents our royalty.

Goode Ol' Warhol

An editted portrait of my teacher created in the style of Andy Warhol. In my Graphic Design class, we learned about Andy Warhol and his art style. Trying to manipulate the picture in Photoshop to recreate his gritty, contemporary style taught me more about working with the filters and effects in the program.

80th Anniversary of Marketing Education

Logo design created for Marketing Education. Since our school is a technical academy, marketing is closely tied with our education. We are trained to appeal to clients and our school emulates the working environment. I researched about what images could symbolize the prestige and classic elements of marketing and education. We also had to give a presentation of the logo and design process to the client.

Anatomy of Type

A poster created to help the Freshmen Graphic Design students understand the different components of typography. I did extensive research on what made up the different parts of letters and used a fun movie title to create a diagram of what I found.

Color in Nature

A digital painting created from a reference photo. Rather than using the standard colors of the photo, the piece is broken up into different color schemes, such as monochromatic, complementary, and analogous. This project helped me learn more about color theory, which colors worked well together and which didn't.

SkillsUSA Promotional Bulletin Board

A design that my teammate and I created for the Promotional Bulletin Board competition at the SkillsUSA State in Reno, Nevada. We worked for several months to create and build a bulletin board as well as pamphlets describing our local chapter and a 10-min pitch to a panel of judges. This won Bronze at the 2015 SkillsUSA Competition.

LVGEA Logo

Logo design created for the 60th anniversary of the Las Vegas Global Economic Alliance. LVGEA was my first client for graphic design work. I had to research the company and learn more about the brand before creating a logo to represent their 60 years of innovative success.

Self Portrait

A digital self portrait using vectors. This my first large-scale project in Adobe Illustrator. This was where I applied the basic skills I learned in Graphic Design to create an artwork. Although vectors don't seem to be the most conventional way to create realistic artwork, it gives a cool digital feel to it.
